Mechanism of Action

Metformin works primarily by inhibiting hepatic glucose production (gluconeogenesis) in the liver. It activates AMP-activated protein kinase (AMPK), which improves insulin sensitivity and reduces intestinal glucose absorption. Unlike sulfonylureas, it does not stimulate insulin secretion and is not associated with hypoglycemia when used as monotherapy.

Is Metformin safe to take with blood pressure medication?
✦
Generally yes — most antihypertensives are compatible with Metformin. ACE inhibitors and ARBs are actually commonly co-prescribed for diabetic patients since they also protect kidneys. However, if you're taking a diuretic, watch for dehydration, which could affect kidney function and how Metformin is cleared. Always confirm with your prescriber before combining.
